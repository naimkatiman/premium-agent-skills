# Originality checklist (mandatory pre-generation gate)

Run this before generating any mascot. If any box fails, stop and regenerate or
ask for a new direction. The goal is a genuinely new visual system, not
"<product> but with a different pet".

## Reject-on-sight prompts

- "Make it like Copilot / ChatGPT / Clippy / <product mascot>."
- "Same face as <product>, just change the color."
- "Copy <logo> but tweak it slightly."

Response: decline the clone, offer an original alternative, and proceed only with
an independent brief.

## Divergence requirements (all four must differ from any well-known mascot)

| Axis | Requirement |
|---|---|
| Silhouette | A distinct overall outline. Not a recognizable existing badge shape. |
| Palette | Your own color system. Not a one-to-one lift of a known brand palette. |
| Facial structure | Original eye/face geometry and spacing. No copied "assistant face". |
| Motion language | Original timing and signature gestures, not a known mascot's idle. |

## Naming and trade dress

- No third-party product/brand name in concept names, file names, skill output,
  or badges, except in a clearly comparative, nominative sense.
- Avoid trade-dress confusion: do not imply affiliation, sponsorship, or that the
  asset is an official mark of any company.
- Microsoft and others treat logos, icons, designs, and trade dress as
  proprietary brand assets. Originality is the safe path.

## Required output artifact: the divergence note

Every generated mascot ships with a short note, e.g.:

> Divergence: hexagonal-aperture silhouette (not a rounded chat badge); teal→violet
> palette of our own; wide-set lens eyes with a single bridge accent; idle is a
> 2.4s breathing shimmer rather than a bounce. No resemblance to a known product
> mascot intended or implied.

## Asset licensing hygiene

- All mascot assets in this repo are original works authored for the project.
- Any third-party font or example asset must carry its own license; do not vendor
  unlicensed assets.
- Contributors may submit only assets they have the right to license under the
  repo's terms (MIT for code; assets under the same unless a separate asset
  license is added).
